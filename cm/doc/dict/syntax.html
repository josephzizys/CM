Dictionary Syntax

Dictionary entries are documented in a special typographic format:

  • Defined terms are printed in fixed-width bold face and literals and program code are printed in fixed-width face:

    cents->scaler

  • Variables and metavariables (names that stand for pieces of code) are printed in italic face:

    number

  • Terms separated by | are exclusive choices:

    number | boolean

  • Braces { } and brackets [ ] are sometimes used to associate or group the enclosed terms but never actually appear in code:
    • Braces simply group the enclosed terms. For example

      {number | boolean}

      means a single entity, either a number or a boolean value.
    • Brackets mean that the enclosed terms are optional. An optional term may appear at most one time. For example,

      (odds prob [true] [false])

      means that upto two optional values may follow the first:

      (odds 1)
      (odds 0 'ouch)
      (odds .2 "Yup" "Nope")

  • A star * after a brace means that the enclosed terms may be specified zero or more times. For example,

    (new class {slot value}*)

    means that any number of slot and value pairs, including none, may follow the required class argument:

    (new midi)
    (new midi :time 10)
    (new midi :keynum 22 :duration 3)

  • A plus + after a brace means that the enclosed terms may be specified one or more times.
  • A sequence of three dots means the rest of the arguments. For example,

    (pick arg ...)

    means that any number of additional values may follow arg.
  • Lisp keyword symbols (symbols that start with a colon) are highlighted in red. Keyword symbols may appear as values or may define keyword arguments to functions, that is, optional arguments that consist of a keyword name followed by its value. For example:

    (hertz x [:in scale])

    defines :in to be a keyword argument to the hertz function:

    (hertz 220)
    (hertz 330 :in myscale)
